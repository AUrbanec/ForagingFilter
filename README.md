# 🍄 Reddit Foraging Filter

A simple Google Chrome extension that automatically hides posts in the `r/foraging` subreddit that contain specific words. 

If you are tired of seeing endless posts about "ramps" or "morels," this extension will make them disappear from your feed! You can easily turn words on or off, or add your own custom words to block.

## ✨ Features
* **Built-in Filters:** Comes pre-loaded to hide posts containing "ramps," "morels," "wild carrot," and "wild garlic."
* **Customizable:** Add any new word you want to block.
* **Easy Toggles:** Check or uncheck a box to turn a filter on or off instantly.
* **Works as you scroll:** Automatically keeps hiding posts even as you scroll down the page.

## 📥 How to Download and Install

Because this extension isn't in the official Chrome Web Store yet, you will need to install it manually. Don't worry, it only takes about 60 seconds! 

### Step 1: Download the files
1. Look for the green **"<> Code"** button near the top right of this page.
2. Click it, then click **"Download ZIP"** from the drop-down menu.
3. Once downloaded, find the `.zip` file on your computer (usually in your Downloads folder). 
4. **Right-click** the file and select **"Extract All..."** (Windows) or double-click it (Mac) to unzip the folder. 
   > ⚠️ *Important: Save this unzipped folder somewhere safe on your computer where you won't accidentally delete it! Chrome needs this folder to stay exactly where it is for the extension to work.*

### Step 2: Add it to Chrome
1. Open Google Chrome.
2. Type `chrome://extensions/` into the web address bar at the top and press **Enter**.
3. In the top-right corner of the page, turn on the switch labeled **"Developer mode"**.
4. In the top-left corner, a new button will appear called **"Load unpacked"**. Click it.
5. A window will pop up. Find and select the folder you unzipped in Step 1, then click **Select Folder**.

That's it! The extension is now installed. 🎉

## 🛠️ How to Use It

**1. Pin the Extension (Optional but recommended)**
To easily access your filters, click the gray "puzzle piece" icon 🧩 in the top right corner of Chrome. Find **Foraging Filter** in the list and click the "Pin" icon next to it. 

**2. Manage your blocked words**
* Click the extension icon in your toolbar to open the menu.
* **To turn off a word:** Uncheck the box next to it.
* **To delete a word permanently:** Click the red **X**.
* **To add a new word:** Type it into the box at the bottom and click **Add**.

**3. Browse Reddit!**
Just go to `r/foraging` or scroll your home feed. The extension works automatically in the background. If you change your filters while looking at Reddit, just refresh the page for the changes to take effect.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Chrome is asking me to "Disable developer mode extensions." What do I do?**
Because you installed this outside of the official Google store, Chrome might occasionally show a pop-up warning you to disable it. This is normal! You can just click "Cancel" or the "X" to dismiss the warning. 

**Why isn't it hiding a post?**
Double-check that the word is spelled correctly in your filter menu and that the checkbox next to it is checked. (Note: The filter isn't case-sensitive, so "Ramps" and "ramps" work exactly the same).

**I accidentally deleted the folder on my computer, and now the extension is broken!**
Chrome reads the extension directly from the folder on your computer. If you moved or deleted the folder, the extension will break. To fix it, just repeat the installation steps above!
